Parkinson Canada brand and website evolution

2021

Description

Defining a more engaging, consistent, and digital-first visual brand identity for Parkinson Canada, implemented through a new homepage design.

Tools used:

  • Adobe: Photoshop, Illustrator
  • CMS: WordPress

Background

Parkinson Canada collects donations to advocate and provide supports for Canadians living with Parkinson's disease, as well as funding projects to help find a cure for Parkinson's. In 2021, the charity deubted their new brand voice, No Matter What, which launched with only two visual assets: a logo and a video. The video showed various individuals against a black background sharing their account of strength and resilience after being diagnosed with Parkinson's.

Process – before and after

Art direction

Screencaps from the video and the shoot were used to create graphics, but the look and feel of the video was very stark and serious and did not reflect the hopeful and uplifting direction Parkinson Canada was taking. Older photos were sometimes used to fill this gap, but were not ideal because they included old brand imagery or slogans.

The organization wanted more energy and optimism in No Matter What's visual identity but needed to do more with less.

To keep consistent with Parkinson Canada's existing visual identity and brand palette (red, white, shades of grey) while being innovative and evoking the energy and dynamism missing from No Matter What's existing visual assets, I developed the art direction of having grayscale images over a red "wave" background. The images could variably be photos of Canadians living with Parkinson's or illustrations I created, as needed.

Website

I redesigned the homepage of Parkinson Canada's website to include the art direction I developed for No Matter What, introduce more colour and imagery, more balanced image-to-text proportions, as well as iconographic UI elements.

I focused the imagery on people to provide compelling visual storytelling and told a story through the layout of the homepage itself, introducing the challenge faced by the hero (the donor) and emphasizing the donor's role and impact.

Contrast between foreground and background in UI elements was also increased to comply with Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG), per the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA).
